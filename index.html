What this demonstrates

An interactive study of connectome harmonics on real human data — the HCP group-average structural connectome plus a 12-subject LSD fMRI cohort (OpenNeuro ds003059). Three questions are tested, each with a result you can inspect below:

  1. 1. Does LSD reorganize the brain's harmonic spectrum? Low-frequency harmonics lose power under LSD (LSD/placebo ≈ 0.77) — the direction reported by Atasoy 2017. see the spectra ↓
  2. 2. Is there a geometric “latent space” in the brain? Yes — the seven functional networks separate into a continuous layout in harmonic coordinates, with no labels given to the algorithm. explore it ↓
  3. 3. Shape vs wiring — which explains activity, and does LSD change it? All bases are close; cortical shape (and a distance surrogate) do at least as well as wiring. Under LSD the geometric basis gains on the connectome. see the curves ↓

What this simulates

Nodes are brain regions; edges are structural connections. The normalized graph Laplacian turns connectivity into a geometry. Its eigenvectors vk are spatial harmonics — the connectome’s allowed standing-wave patterns.

xi(t) = ak vk,i cos(√λk · t)

This demo animates those basis patterns. Real fMRI activity can be decomposed into the same harmonics (see literature below).

Geometric latent space

The harmonics aren't just waves — they're coordinate axes. Each region gets a position (va,i, vb,i) in a low-dimensional embedding (this is a Laplacian eigenmap / diffusion-map latent space). If a smooth geometry exists, the seven Yeo functional networks should separate into a continuous layout — without ever telling the algorithm what they are.

Load the HCP structural SC dataset (top) to explore its latent space — the toy graph has no network labels.

LSD & connectome harmonics

The paper Atasoy et al. 2017 (Scientific Reports) — often discussed as “connectome-specific harmonic waves under LSD” — does not publish a downloadable harmonic-power dataset. It reports findings from decomposing LSD/placebo fMRI:

↓ Low-frequency harmonics (broad modes like those at the start of the tour) contribute less under LSD.

↑ High-frequency harmonics (fine-grained modes) contribute more.

Expanded repertoire — more distinct harmonic brain states become active, with correlated cross-frequency co-activation.

Geometry vs connectivity

Is brain activity shaped more by the connectome's wiring or by the cortex's shape? Following Pang et al. 2023, we reconstruct the ds003059 fMRI from the first N vectors of three Schaefer-400 bases and measure accuracy vs N: connectome (wiring), geometric (cortical shape, Laplace–Beltrami modes), and EDR (a distance-only surrogate).

— connectome — geometric — EDR

Caveat: parcellated (400 regions) — the regime where the geometric advantage is weakest; geometric modes are hemisphere-separable while connectome/EDR are whole-brain. The LSD shift is significance-tested (within-subject paired test, see above), but the broader vertex-level geometry-vs-wiring debate remains open (cf. Mansour et al. 2024).

Brain activity rides the connectome's harmonics — and LSD bends which ones

Four reproducible experiments on real human neuroimaging. We turn the brain's structural wiring into a graph, take the eigenmodes of its graph Laplacian (the connectome's harmonics), project resting-state fMRI onto them, and ask what changes under a psychedelic. Every result below is interactive.

Does the psychedelic brain become more “harmonious”? (STV)

Method: score the consonance / spectral symmetry of the connectome-harmonic spectrum (Sethares dissonance, entropy) — paired test, drug vs placebo.

No — it gets less consonant. Against the naive STV prediction, the harmonic spectrum becomes more dissonant and broader under LSD (the “entropic brain” direction). dissonance ↑ p=0.001d_z=1.24entropy ↑ p=0.007holds after GSR + permpsilocybin: same direction, n.s.

Bottom line: under LSD the harmonic repertoire reorganizes — less low-frequency, wiring-bound structure and a measurable drift toward smooth cortical-shape patterns. The psychedelic shift is a within-subject effect significant at p<0.001 across all 12 subjects, and it survives nuisance regression (global-signal + detrend + motion-proxy censoring) and an exact permutation null — so it is not merely a motion or global-signal artifact. It then replicates in direction in an independent psilocybin cohort (ds006072, n=7: same geometry-gains shift, d_z=0.87, exact-permutation p=0.031) — a different drug, scanner, and pipeline. Separately, a harmonic-consonance probe (a test of the Symmetry Theory of Valence) finds the spectrum becomes less consonant and broader under LSD (p=0.001, survives GSR + permutation) — the opposite of a naive “psychedelic = more symmetry” reading, and a reminder that “drifting toward the smooth geometric basis” is not the same as “becoming more consonant.” It remains a coarse, parcellated result, and the psilocybin replication is underpowered (n=7, active placebo).

Geometry vs connectivity

Is brain activity shaped more by the connectome's wiring or by the cortex's shape? Following Pang et al. 2023, we reconstruct the ds003059 fMRI from the first N vectors of three Schaefer-400 bases and measure accuracy vs N: connectome (real wiring, incl. long-range tracts), geometric (cortical shape, Laplace–Beltrami modes), and EDR (connectivity that decays with distance only — geometry-as-a-network, not real wiring). So the headline "toward geometry, away from wiring" is the geometry − connectome contrast; the geometry − EDR contrast compares two flavours of geometry and is a different question.

— connectome — geometric — EDR

Caveat: parcellated (400 regions) — the regime where the geometric advantage is weakest; geometric modes are hemisphere-separable while connectome/EDR are whole-brain. The LSD shift is significance-tested (within-subject paired test + exact sign-flip permutation), survives nuisance regression (GSR + detrend + DVARS censoring), and replicates in direction on an independent psilocybin cohort (ds006072, n=7: Δ=+0.010, d_z=0.87, exact-permutation p=0.031; marginal after GSR) — so it is not merely a motion/global-signal artifact or a single-dataset fluke. A vertex-resolution check (fsaverage5-10k) confirms the geometric basis reconstructs activity efficiently off the parcellation, but also shows — honestly — that a fair geometry-vs-wiring test is not achievable at vertex resolution with public data (only a synthetic connectome surrogate exists), and that the geometry-vs-distance contrast shifts the other way at fine scale (Δgeo−EDR=−0.007, p=0.031): the effect is specific to the geometry-vs-wiring contrast. Remaining limits: no fmriprep motion parameters on the LSD raw dataset, modest cohorts (n=12 LSD, n=7 psilocybin with an active placebo), and the broader vertex-level geometry-vs-wiring debate stays open (cf. Mansour et al. 2024).

Harmonic consonance & the symmetry theory of valence

A natural follow-up: does the degree of geometric/harmonic order correlate with positive affect? The Symmetry Theory of Valence (Johnson 2023) proposes that how pleasant an experience feels tracks the symmetry of its neural representation; the Qualia Research Institute operationalizes this on connectome harmonics via consonance. We can't test the affect correlation directly — neither dataset ships per-subject mood ratings — but we can test the mechanism direction: does a psychedelic move the harmonic spectrum toward consonance/symmetry, or away?

For each subject and state we project BOLD onto the connectome harmonics, take the power per mode, and score the spectrum's Sethares dissonance (lower = more consonant), its spectral entropy (breadth), and its spectral centroid (high vs low spatial frequency), then run the same within-subject paired test as Experiment 3.

▸ How these numbers are computed (entropy, centroid, dissonance)

Everything starts from one object — the harmonic power spectrum of a scan — and each metric is just a different summary of it.

Step 0 — the spectrum. The fMRI is a matrix Y of 400 regions × time, z-scored per region. We project every frame onto the connectome harmonics (the orthonormal eigenvectors vk of the graph Laplacian): C = Vᵀ·Y, so C[k,t] is "how much of mode k is active at time t." The power of mode k is its time-averaged squared coefficient, pk = meant C[k,t]², normalized so Σ pk = 1. So p is a distribution over the harmonics: what fraction of this brain's activity lives at each spatial frequency. Each mode also has a frequency ωk = √λk (low mode = smooth/global, high mode = fine-grained; the √ is the drum-pitch analogy).

The three metrics (each one line of p and ω):

  • Spectral entropy = −Σ pk ln pk / ln K — Shannon entropy of p on a 0–1 scale. Near 0 = power packed into a few modes; near 1 = spread evenly across many (a broad repertoire). It only measures how spread out the power is.
  • Spectral centroid = Σ pk ωk — the power-weighted average frequency, the spectrum's "center of mass." Higher = energy has moved into finer, higher-frequency modes.
  • Consonance / dissonance — the only metric that looks at relationships between modes. We treat the spectrum as a musical chord: each mode is a tone with frequency fk and loudness ak = √pk. For every pair of modes we add the Sethares (1993) "sensory dissonance," a curve that is 0 for identical tones, peaks when two tones are close but not equal (they "beat," like two out-of-tune notes), then fades when they're far apart. Total over all pairs = dissonance (lower = more consonant). Because that curve is calibrated in Hz, we rescale the ω's into the audible range (lowest mode → 220 Hz) — this keeps all frequency ratios and is identical across conditions, so it can't bias the LSD-vs-placebo comparison.

Entropy and dissonance both rise when power spreads out, but they differ: entropy ignores where the modes sit, while dissonance specifically penalizes power landing in neighbouring, "beating" modes. Each per-scan number then goes through the same paired drug-vs-placebo test as Experiment 3. Full code: harmonic_consonance.py.

Result — against the naive prediction. Under LSD the harmonic spectrum becomes more dissonant (Δ=+16.7, paired p=0.001, exact-permutation p=0.002, d_z=1.24), broader (entropy p=0.007) and shifted toward higher spatial frequencies (centroid p=0.015); all survive global-signal regression. Psilocybin (n=7) trends the same way but is not significant. This is the “entropic brain” direction — the opposite of a simple “psychedelic bliss = more symmetry/consonance” reading of STV.

Note this is not in tension with Experiment 3: activity can drift toward the smooth geometric basis relative to wiring while the overall spectrum still spreads to higher modes — “more geometric alignment” ≠ “more consonance.” The consonance metric is the sharper STV variable.

Caveats: this tests the mechanism direction, not the affect correlation (no per-subject valence labels exist in either release); it measures spatial-harmonic consonance, whereas QRI's “neural annealing” predicts a transient rise in energy/entropy before annealing, so an acute broadening is not a clean refutation of STV; n=12/7, parcellated, and the spatial-frequency→audio mapping is a modelling choice (the direction is robust across raw, GSR, and permutation, and for LSD across both the connectome and geometric harmonic substrates).
